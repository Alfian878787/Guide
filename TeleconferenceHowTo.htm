Setting up a Teleconference

This Guidebook is the collected wisdom of the W3C Group Chairs and other collaborators.

As of June 2015 W3C teleconferences moved to the MIT instance of Cisco WebEx. Most Team Contacts are able to schedule meetings directly in WebEx; ask your Team Contact for assistance.

If your Team Contact is unavailable and it is urgent, you may send mail to adminreq@w3.org with the following information:.

  1. (optional but helpful) include "WebEx" or "teleconference" in the subject line of the mail
  2. Day(s): Day of week
  3. Date(s): YYYY-MM-DD *see advance notice
    Time: HH:MM EST (Boston Time) duration HH:MM hours
  4. One Time or Recurring *see recurring timezone
  5. (optional) Password: xxx *see password

Requests should be made at least 48 hours in advance of your meeting date. You will receive a confirmation reply with call details. To send the details to the participants, please cut and paste the confirmation details. Retyping the information is prone to frustrating errors.

Notes

*advance notice
  • 2 business days
*who can reserve a teleconference
W3C staff, Chairs of Working, Interest, and Business Groups.
*recurring timezone
Conferences are normally scheduled in Boston local time and the absolute UTC time will shift forward or backward when the US changes its clocks. If you wish the conference to be scheduled relative to some other timezone and shift in UTC when that timezone shifts, make a special note of it in your request. Nota bene: if your conference time shifts on different calendar dates than most other teleconferences (i.e. not on the US schedule) your participants may encounter conflicts they had not anticipated.
*meeting password
Callers who join the meeting on-line by entering the meeting number may be prompted to enter a password. If you wish a password other than "w3c" you should specify your password. Passwords with only lowercase letters and numbers are easiest for mobile users to type.

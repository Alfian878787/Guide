Working Groups

Working Groups are the heart of the W3C Process. The W3C Team spends a certain amount of resources reviewing Submissions, running Workshops, and otherwise tracking Web developments, which culminates in the process of re-allocating W3C resources:

  1. The Director announces the development of a proposal for one or more new Interest Group or Working Group charters.
  2. Advisory Committee representatives review the proposed charters.
  3. The Director announces a decision to approve the group(s) (or otherwise), as proposed or with modifications suggested by reviewers.

Working Groups and Interest Groups produce Recommendations and other technical reports, and sample code.

For information about participating in a Working Group or Interest Group with participation requirements that exceed mailing list subscription, please refer to these forms for joining a group. For information about participating in an Interest Group where the only participation requirement is mailing list subscription, see information on mailing list administrativia.

Member Communications

Charter Proposals, Calls for Participation, and Calls for Review are disseminated from the Director to the Advisory Committee via email (w3c-ac-members archive). The weekly NewsWire and monthly Newsletter summarize these official communications, among other items of interest. If you don't receive these communications, see the section on Member Mailing Lists in the Introduction for New Members.

Responses from your organization must come through your Advisory Committee representative. As a supplement to Advisory Committee Meetings, AC representatives are invited to participate in w3c-ac-forum.

The Art of Consensus is a guidebook that documents conventional wisdoms among Working Group Chairs, Editors, etc. Working Group Chairs discuss W3C process and other issues in the chairs@w3.org forum.
